Causes of Delays

  1. Weather Conditions
    • Severe weather such as thunderstorms, snowstorms, fog, or high winds can disrupt airport operations and air traffic routes. Even weather in a different city can delay your flight due to aircraft positioning.
  2. Aircraft Maintenence
    • Airlines perform routine and unscheduled maintenance checks to ensure safety. While inconvenient, these delays prioritize passenger well-being and regulatory compliance.
  3. Air Traffic Control
    • Busy airspace, congestion at major hubs, or staffing limitations can result in ground holds or extended taxi times.
  4. Crew Availability
    • Crews are subject to strict duty-time regulations. If a crew exceeds their allowed working hours due to earlier delays, a replacement may be required.
